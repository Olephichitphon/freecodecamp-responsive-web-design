Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

A timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa.
  • 1933 - Enrolled at the University of Minnesota to study forestry.
  • 1944 - Began his career at the Rockefeller Foundation's agricultural research program in Mexico.
  • 1960s - Developed high-yield, disease-resistant dwarf wheat varieties, which helped to prevent famine in Mexico, Pakistan, and India.
  • 1970 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his revolutionary work in agriculture.
  • 2009 - Passed away at the age of 95.
